Who I Am


My name is Thomas, and I have always been interested in infrastructure of all kinds! You might say that growing up in New York City, I have witnessed firsthand how crucial infrastructure is keeping any village, town, or city running. I love that, as a civil engineer, I can impact the built environment all around me and make a positive difference in the lives of hundreds, thousands, and even millions of people. While I find all facets of civil engineering fascinating, my main areas of interests currently lie in structural engineering and transportation engineering. Most of my coursework has been directly relevant to structural engineering, and I recently interned as a transportation engineering intern to find out more about the industry.

I am currently an undergraduate senior studying towards a Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering at Cornell University. I cannot wait to start making an impact in the world upon graduation, no matter where in civil engineering I may end up!

2021-2022 Steel Bridge Competition Video

During the 2021-2022 Student Steel Bridge Competition, the Cornell University Steel Bridge Project Team participated in the virtual competition for that year. The team designed and analyzed an entire 20-foot long using MASTAN2 and Fusion 360, considered strategies for constructing it in a live-competition setting, and presented it a 10-minute pitch video detailing why the bridge should be strongly considered the winner. I assisted in the design of the bridge and was placed in charge of directing, shooting, and editing the final pitch video posted to YouTube. Our submission won 1st Place in the Video awards category.


The Downfall of New York City's Subway (Research Paper)

In a fall semester course I took my sophomore year, CRP 3860: Sustainable Transportation, I learned a ton about carbon footprints, modalities of transportation, problems surrounding transportation today, and much more. For our final essay assignment, we were tasked to explore a topic regarding transportation sustainability that we were genuinely curious to learn more about. Being from New York City, I firsthand have witnessed the state that the New York City Subway is in and have experienced numerous delays stemming from a run-down system. Yet, I never know why this was the case. In this final paper, I explored why and how the New York City Subway reached the state it is in today, how these shortcomings impact the daily lives of New Yorkers, and compared it to more successful foreign subway systems.


Three-Story Concrete Frame Building (Semester Design Project)

One of the most intensive courses in the Civil Engineering major here at Cornell University, CEE 4730: Design of Concrete Structures was a semester design class where students would learn the basics of structural concrete in lecture, test for different modes of failure via structural beams with differing amounts of reinforcing steel in Bovay Lab, and design an entire three-story concrete building in our own time. My group of four, affectionately named Concrete Jungle Consulting, worked relentlessly to design one-way slabs, beams, shear cores, and columns all out of reinforced concrete. The entire time, we maintained a professional atmosphere via thorough, professional documentation and drawings from AutoCAD.


Climate Change and Earth Damn Failures (Research Paper and Presentation)

In CEE 3410: Introduction to Geotechnical Engineering, we learned tons about the very soils that form the foundations of everything we build. The breadth of topics covered was large as topics that were covered include: material characterization, weight-volume relationships, water flows and capillary actions, soil stresses, compaction, settlement, and more. In Bovay Lab, we gained firsthand experience with using sieves, learned more about the charactersitics of dry and saturated weights of soils, and performed standardized soil tests. Towards the end of the semester, my group and I performed some research involving the impacts of climate change on earthen dams. We presented our results in the form of a research paper and presentation.

Education

Cornell University


At Cornell University, I am majoring in Civil Engineering and minoring in Mechanical Engineering. I have taken numerous courses regarding different facets of civil engineering, including geotechnical engineering, transportation engineering, and structural engineering. With a focus on structural engineering, I have taken a significant number of structural-based courses, such as structural design courses involving reinforced concrete, steel, and masonry. To support my learning of structural engineering, I have been involved with the Cornell University Steel Bridge Project Team ("Cornell Steel Bridge") since October of my freshman year and have held a leadership role for three of my four years being on the team (Welding Lead for two years, currently Team Lead for one year). Steel Bridge found tremendous success in 2021-2022 when our bridge won 3rd Place overall in our regionals competition. In addition to my numerous academics, I am also involved with the upper-division men's ultimate frisbee team, the Cornell Buds, since my freshman year of college. We travel up and down the East Coast playing at tournaments to grow and hone our teamwork and ultimate frisbee skills.
